Multiple Decisions

In Python, if statements let you do things some of the time. For example, the code below only makes a stamp if the turtle has traveled 30 steps (to keep the stamps from overlapping):

Let's step through the program:

  1. We create a turtle for drawing.
  2. For each item in the list,
    1. Move forward the distance num from our list,
    2. Turn our turtle to the right 93 degrees, and
    3. If num is bigger than 30, we stamp a turtle shape.

The variable, num, takes each value in the list of the numbers. We use it to move forward num steps and we also check it's size to decide whether to stamp (or not).
