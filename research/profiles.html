Sample Research Profiles

Michael Hintze, a junior from Queens, is a mathematics major and member of the Macaulay Honors College. He is part of an interdisciplinary team focusing on understanding phylogenetic treespaces (all possible evolutionary histories for a set of species) and improving searches for the optimal phylogenetic tree. His research is being supported by the Louis Stokes Alliance for Minority Participation in Research and Education (LSAMP) and National Science Foundation grant awarded to his mentor, Prof. Katherine St. John. This summer, he has been accepted to the prestiguous Research In Industrial Projects for Students (RIPS) at the Institute for Pure and Applied Mathematics (IPAM) at UCLA, where he will work in a team on a real-world research project. At Lehman, he is also a member of the mens varsity soccer team. After graduation, he is considering graduate school in applied mathematics or a career in finance.

Alan Joseph Caceres, a sophomore from the Bronx, is a computer science major. He is part of an interdisciplinary team focusing on understanding phylogenetic treespaces (all possible evolutionary histories for a set of species) and improving searches for the optimal phylogenetic tree. He has developed algorithms to search for minimal paths through treespaces. His poster on this work won a second prize award at the poster competition at the FGL-LSAMP Expo 2010 help in February at the University of South Florida. His research is being supported by the Louis Stokes Alliance for Minority Participation in Research and Education (LSAMP) and National Science Foundation grant awarded to his mentor, Prof. Katherine St. John. Through the CUNY Institute of Software Design and Development, Alan has also been an intern in the public schools, working on improving technology in the classroom. After graduation, he plans to go to graduate school in computer science.
