  • Description:  Computers have become an integral part of our daily lives.  In this seminar, we look at the effects of computers on society, in particular the incorporation and long-reaching consequences of robotics on how we interact with the world.  To give depth and context to this discussion, the course includes a hands-on component of designing and programming Legos Mindstorm Robots.  The basics of locomotion, design, as well as introductory programming (in a variant of the popular C-programming language, customized for the robots) will be taught.  As part of the seminar, students will design and implement a robotics project of their choosing.  Students in the course will write traditional essays, as well as a technical guides to using their robotics projects.
  • Textbook: There are two required textbooks:
    • Annual Editions: Computers in Society
    • Dave Baum's Definitive Guide to Lego Mindstorm Robots
  • Grading: The grading for the course will be based on:
    • Computer Laboratory Exercises: 30%
    • Written Homework & Quizzes: 30%
    • Class Participation: 10% 
    • Final Project: 30%
  • Computer Laboratory Exercises:  Laboratory exercises are posted on the class website, usually the week before class.  They reinforce concepts covered in lecture and should be completed within 3 weeks of the original laboratory.  Note that has the semester progresses, the exercises will take design and programming outside of class to complete.  To receive credit for an laboratory exercise, you must demonstrate the working robot, the associated programs, and explain how they work.
  • Written Homework & Quizzes: There will be written homework and quizzes based on the the readings for class.  In general, the first assignment on any reading will be due the Wednesday before it's discussed in class.  We will use the on-line Blackboard system for both written exercises and quizzes.  You can resubmit written homework and quizzes for regrading within 3 weeks of the original due date.  See the course webpage for more details about homework and quizzes.    
  • Project:  This course will have a significant project of your own design or a 15-20 page term paper.  Your project should include a robot designed to solve a given problem, associated programs, and a webpage documenting how to use and maintain your robot.  More information about the project will be available on the course website. 
  • Honor Code: You are encouraged to work together on the overall design of the programs and homework. However, for specific programs and homework assignments, all work must be your own.  You are responsible for knowing and following Lehman's academic integrity code (available from the Undergraduate Bulletin, Graduate Bulletin, or the Office of Academic Standards and Evaluations).
    All incidents of cheating will be reported to the Vice President of Student Affairs.
  • Computer Access: Part of this course will use university computer laboratories.  These machines are for work related to this course only and a code of conduct applies to computer use in the department and on-campus.  Misusing university computers could result in losing your computer access for the rest of the term, making it exceedingly difficult to complete this course.
