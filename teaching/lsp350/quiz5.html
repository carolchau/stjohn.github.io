Format

Quiz 2 consists of two parts:
  1. A multiple choice quiz on the reading, and
  2. A short essay based on the reading.
For Part 1, the Blackboard system randomly chooses from a pool of questions to make the quiz.  If you are not happy with your score, you can take the quiz over, and a new set of random questions will be generated.

For Part 2, you need to enter your essay answer to the chosen question.  Since there's several to choose from, make sure to clearly mark at the beginning which question you have chosen to answer.  Spelling and grammar do count, so, please use a spell-checker before entering your essay.  Since Blackboard will only accept plain text, it is probably easiest to write your essay in a word processor and then cut-and-paste it into the quiz window.

Topics

The questions in Part 1 are straightforward and based on the reading.  The best way to study for this is to do the reading.

For Part 2, choose one of the following questions and write a short essay.  Your essay should be about a page long and clearly address the question.  The questions below are from p 76 of the text:

1. The overview of this unit mentions Alexis de Tocqueville's observation that Americans tend to form civic associations and Robert Putman's argument that the tendency is declining.  Do you think that computing has played any part in the decline?  What does Putnam say?  Wat do other scholars say about Putnam's work?

2. Howard Rheingold, in "Is that a Computer in Your Pansts?" says, "we're entering a world in which the complexity of the devices and the system of interconnecting devices is beyond our capacity to easily understand."  Is this cause for alarm?  Can anything be done about it?  What are your suggestions?

3. After reading "Why Women Avoid Computer Science," why do you think so few women study computing?
