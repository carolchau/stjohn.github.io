Format

Quiz 2 consists of two parts:
  1. A multiple choice quiz on the reading, and
  2. A short essay based on the reading.
For Part 1, the Blackboard system randomly chooses from a pool of questions to make the quiz.  If you are not happy with your score, you can take the quiz over, and a new set of random questions will be generated.

For Part 2, you need to enter your essay answer to the chosen question.  Since there's several to choose from, make sure to clearly mark at the beginning which question you have chosen to answer.  Spelling and grammar do count, so, please use a spell-checker before entering your essay.  Since Blackboard will only accept plain text, it is probably easiest to write your essay in a word processor and then cut-and-paste it into the quiz window.

Topics

The questions in Part 1 are straightforward and based on the reading.  The best way to study for this is to do the reading.

For Part 2, choose one of the following questions and write a short essay.  Your essay should be about a page long and clearly address the question.  The questions below are from p 102 of the text:

1. The overview of this unit mentions that civil institutions were overlooked in the excitement after the collapse of the former Soviet Union.  On the Internet, find and read Francis Fukuyama's essay, "The End of History."  Do you agree with his arguments?   Does computing have any role to play in the development of civil institutions?

2. Napster and its descendents have been much in the news lately.  Do you agree with the recording industry position that downloading music is copyright infringement?  Why or why not?

3. At least one state has replaced polling places with main-in voting.  The next step would be to replace main-in voting with the Internet.  How would this affect democracy in the United States?
