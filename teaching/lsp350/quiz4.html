Target Date:  19 October 2005
Reading:  Unit 3: Work and the Workplace in Annual Editions 

Format

Quiz 2 consists of two parts:
  1. A multiple choice quiz on the reading, and
  2. A short essay based on the reading.
For Part 1, the Blackboard system randomly chooses from a pool of questions to make the quiz.  If you are not happy with your score, you can take the quiz over, and a new set of random questions will be generated.

For Part 2, you need to enter your essay answer to the chosen question.  Since there's several to choose from, make sure to clearly mark at the beginning which question you have chosen to answer.  Spelling and grammar do count, so, please use a spell-checker before entering your essay.  Since Blackboard will only accept plain text, it is probably easiest to write your essay in a word processor and then cut-and-paste it into the quiz window.

Topics

The questions in Part 1 are straightforward and based on the reading.  The best way to study for this is to do the reading.

For Part 2, choose one of the following questions and write a short essay.  Your essay should be about a page long and clearly address the question.  The questions below are from p 42 of the text:

1. The issue on immigration to the United States is controversial. Do you agree with AnnaLee Saxenian's arguments? Why or why not?

2. In "You're Hired, Now Go Home," Jeanne Allert cites four traits to look for when hiring an individual to do virtual work. What traits would you add?

3. Suppose you learned that your university is reading sutdent e-mail to protect itself from sexual harassment suites and its students from sexual harassment itself. Have the course said anything about a university's rights and obligations in these matters? What is the policy at CUNY? Do you think it is appropriate?
