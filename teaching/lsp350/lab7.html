Locomotion & Gears

Today's lab focuses on using using gears and different ways to make your robot move.  In particular, we'll look at how gearing can improve speed and torque.

Design requirements: In this lab, we will build and test by hand (but not attached to the robots) the concepts about gearing and locomotion in Chapter 4.  For this part, you will need a piece of string in addition to the standard lego parts.
We will also build robots for a drag-race and for a tug-a-war.

Basic Gearing

The Lego robots come with four standard gears: 8, 16, 24, and 40 teeth gears.  These can be combined  to with our motors to produce either more rotations ("gearing up") or less rotations ("gearing down").  This first part of the lab asks you to figure about which combination of gears produce more or less rotations and what is there ratio.

Take a piece of string and attach a couple of legos to one end.  Attach two 16-tooth gears to one of the black beams using medium length black bars.  The gears should mesh when you attach them to the black beam.  On the other end of the black bars attach grey circle plates (one will be used as a handle).  See p 73 of the Baum textbook.  Turn the handle-- the second gear should move the same speed. 
  • What happens if you replace the two 16-tooth gears with two 24-tooth gears?  Which pulley (the grey disk) moves faster?  What is the ratio between the speeds?
  • What happens if you use a 8-tooth and 24-tooth gears?  Which pulley (the grey disk) moves faster?  What is the ratio between the speeds?
  • What happens if you use a 24-tooth and 40-tooth gears?  Which pulley (the grey disk) moves faster?  What is the ratio between the speeds?
  • Due to the physical size of the Lego pieces, some of the gearing combinations can't be placed together on the black beam.  Which combinations are those?
Next, build the compound drive train from p 74.  Note that it uses multiple stages of gears to create gear ratios that could be far larger or smaller than available from a single pair of gears. 
  • What is the gear ratio from this compound drive train?
  • Hook this drive train up to a motor and run a simple program that will make the motor move forward.  Try to stop the motor with your hand by holding on to each of the grey disks.  Which disk has greater torque?

Drag Race

Next, build the fastest robot possible.  To do this, you will likely want to try different gearing configuations
and examine the compound drive train you built above.  We will race the robots in the hallway to see whose is fastest.

Tug-a-War

We will also have a tug-a-war, where two robots each are attached to a rope, and they try to pull the other one across the line.  Note to increase the pulling power of your robot, you will have to "gear down" the motors and give up speed.
