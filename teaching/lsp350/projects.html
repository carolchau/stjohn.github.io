Project Information

LSP 350: Computers & Society
Fall 2005
Lehman College-- CUNY


For the project for this course, you have the choice of writing a 15-20 page term paper, expanding on the topics covered in class, or building a robot and designing the accompanying programs.

If you choose to write a term paper:
  • You can expand on one of the essays that you wrote for the quizzes.
  • The use of outside sources is required.  A good place to start is the bibliography of the articles in each unit.
  • A thesis statement and outline needs to be submitted by 30 November, to make sure it's a reasonable topic for a long paper.

If you choose to build a robot:

  • Your project can have up to 5 programs (the total size of all loaded programs cannot exceed 6K).
  • Different lego configurations for different programs are possible, but you must be able to convert from one to another quickly.
  • Design ideas need to be submitted by 1 December, to make sure that they're the right level of difficulty for the project.

Project Ideas

  • a jukebox that plays songs on request
  • a robot with an arm (see the last chapter of Baum's book)
  • a robot that navigates a maze using the light sensor and bumpers
