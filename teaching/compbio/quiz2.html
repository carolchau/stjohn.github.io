Suggested Problems

The following questions are suggested to prepare for the quiz.  They are based on the above reading and should be attempted before starting the quiz.  You do not need to turn in written answers to the questions below.  Instead, you should log on to Blackboard and take the quiz during the time allotted.

Undergraduate Questions

The following questions are for those taking the course for undergraduate credit:
  1. What is the difference between computational biology and bioinformatics?
  2. Give 3 examples from Dr. Kim's article of problems for which both biology and computer science are needed to solve the problem.
  3. What is the central dogma of molecular biology?
  4. What is the chemical mirror image of:  ACCTGT, GGGAAAGACT, AAA?
  5. To which RNA sequences are the above sequences transcribed?
  6. What animo acids are coded for by the above sequences?
  7. What are the steps in a computational biology experiment?

Graduate Questions

In addition to the undergraduate questions, those taking the course for graduate question should also do the following questions:

  1. What other RNA sequences code for the same sequence of proteins in question 5 above?
  2. How many RNA sequences code for Ser Ser Ser Ser?
  3. What's the longest protein sequence you can form that has exactly one RNA sequence that translates to it?  Don't  use either the STOP or START codons when counting the number of sequences.
  4. Why do biologists model systems?
  5. Why should you keep records of all queries you run, not just the succesful ones?
