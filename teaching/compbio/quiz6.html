Suggested Problems

The following questions are suggested to prepare for the quiz.  They are based on the above reading and should be attempted before starting the quiz.  You do not need to turn in written answers to the questions below.  Instead, you should log on to Blackboard and take the quiz during the time allotted.

Undergraduate Questions

The following questions are for those taking the course for undergraduate credit:
  1. Dynamic programming is used for pairwise alignment of sequences.  Is it used for multiple sequence alignment?  Why or why not?
  2. What is a heuristic algorithm?  What is a greedy algorithm?  Give examples of each in terms of alignment algorithms.
  3. How are phylogenetic trees used in multiple sequence alignment algorithms?
  4. What is a motif?  What is a profile?  How do they differ?
  5. What is PROSITE?
  6. What is a homolog, or homologous genes?  What is the difference between orthologs and paralogs?  (Hint: see the beginning of Chapter 8.)

Graduate Questions

In addition to the undergraduate questions, those taking the course for graduate question should also do the following questions:

  1. What is the difference between a scoring matrix, such as BLOSUM62 and a position-specific scoring matrix (PSSM)?
  2. How long does it take to pairwise align two sequences using a dynamic programming algorithm such as Smith-Waterman?
  3. How long does it take to align all possible pairs of n sequences?  How long does it take with a progressive strategy for the heuristic?
  4. How does ClustalW work?
