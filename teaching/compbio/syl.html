  • Description: CMP 464/788: Topics in Computer Science: Introduction to Computational Biology. 4 hours, 4 credits. Prerequisities:  CMP 338.  CMP 337 or 300 level mathematics course suggested.

    This introductory course in computational biology will be aimed at upper division undergraduate or beginning masteres students in computer.  No biological background is assumed.  Biology students with an interest in bioinformatics may also take the course, but the emphasis will be on the computation side: writing programs to efficiently use and extend current bioinformatics tools.

    Topics to include: introduction to molecular biology and bioinformatics, using perl and unix for bioinfomatics applications, multiple sequence alignment and dynamic programming (e.g. Smith-Waterman), assessment of
    sequence alignments, biological databases and search tools (e.g. FASTA and BLAST), and phylogenetic tree reconstruction algorithms (e.g. Maximum Parsimony and Neighbor Joining, using PAUP* and phylip).
    If time allows, the prediction of protein secondary structure and bioinformatics visualization tools will also be covered.

    Programming:  This course will include regular programming assignments, mostly in the perl scripting language, and a large comprehensive project.  No knowledge of perl is assumed, but previous programming experience is
    required. 
  • Textbook: Developing Bioinformatics Computer Skills, by Gibas & Jambeck, O'Reilly.
  • Programming Environment: A large fraction of bioinformatics programs run on Unix systems, so, we will do the majority of our work on Unix systems, including the computational clusters used for teaching at CUNY.
