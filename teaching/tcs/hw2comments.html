Bookkeeping:

General comments on how the homeworks were graded:
  • The homework grading reflects the how the exam in May (the "First Examination") is graded.  In general, the graders on that exam will not infer what you are thinking from what you wrote, but will only give credit for ideas clearly stated on the paper.  So, it is important to state key ideas and assumptions, and at the end of proofs to summarize what you have just shown.
  • Almost full credit was given for the correct idea, but missing some key idea or step.  If we were not able to deduce what you meant from the what you wrote, little credit was given.  Neatness and clarity count!  If we can't read what you wrote, we can't give you credit.

Common Mistaktes:

  • Large number of students constructed the equivalent NFA N' correctly, but did NOT show acceptance of strings was same.  I ended up giving 7/10 for construction (some didn't get that right either) and 8/10 for at least stating they "clearly" accept the same strings.
  • Several students did an induction-style proof on the second hw question, but got into trouble.  They let Bn+1 = Bn B1, then stated since Bn = a^cn and B1= a^c and both are regular, then they're done.  (Two noted they need the same c, so I only too off 1 point). 
  • On the second question, if they missed the point that they would have to be the same multiple, needing memory.  I deducted only 2 points, and wrote the "whole story" for them - make it [a^ (n+1)]c = [a^(n+1)]*, but I think it is a more serious misunderstanding than the points I took off.  (I think some actually had the right idea from diagrams they drew, and just got the notation mixed up...)
