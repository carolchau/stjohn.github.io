Bookkeeping:

General comments on how the homeworks were graded:
  • I deducted 2 or 3 points on the DFAs for errors that included: undefined transitions, “good” strings not accepted, “bad” strings accepted (in both cases more or less special cases), and extraneous states.  I deducted full credit for a combination of errors that led to both “good” strings rejected and “bad” strings accepted.
  • I deducted 2 or 3 points on the proof (just a couple of papers) for not stating that division by 0 is an illegal operation.  (Some people ran all around the “can’t divide if a = b” without clearly stating “why”.)
  • The papers are pretty good – mostly small errors although some people “nickeled-and-dimed” themselves to below 30/40.

Common Mistaktes:

  • Many students had undefined transitions on their DFAs.  They were confused by the unlabelled arcs in NFAs, while all transitions must be labeled in DFAs.  
  • There was some confusion about when to accept the empty string, e.  Many students who correctly accepted it on the “does not contain substring 110” question also incorrectly accepted it on the “has 1 in odd positions” question. On the “odd position” question, many students rejected all even length strings.
