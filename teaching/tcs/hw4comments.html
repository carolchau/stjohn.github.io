  2. Problem 2.21a, p 122.

Comments

The questions were very well done. 
  • Question 2.15 (proof of closure of CFLs under union, concatenation and kleene star) was correctly proved using construction  either of two ways.  
    • Most students constructed a context-free grammar that would generate the “new” language, although a few students constructed a “new” PDA that accepted the “new” language.  Both are fine, but students should state that their construction proves that the language is context-free.  
    • A few students seemed a little confused as to the question:  to prove that the set of languages is closed under the operations, one should show that the language can be generated by a CFG or accepted by a PDA, and therefore context-free.  Some students instead showed that a particular language is closed under the operation by considering the union,etc.of particular strings and showing they were in the language.
  • Virtually everyone received full credit on Question 2.21 (ambiguity of if-then-if-then-else statement as constructed.).
