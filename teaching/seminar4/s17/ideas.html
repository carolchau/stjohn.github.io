Organize the course around 3 or 4 questions that motivate the tools:
  • my neighborhood: who lives there? where did they come from? where do they work? how do they commute? weather data? crime data? 311 data? what does it look like today and how did it get that way?
  • the future of my neighborhood: who will live there (look at building permits, migration rates, birth rates)? what will it look like in the future?
  • the future of NYC: where is the city growing (building permits, census data)? where can more people live (transit, flood plains, current density)?
  • shaping the future: where should more housing be added? commute times in the region? utilities capacity? transit/road capacity? school capacity? where should transit expand? what should happen with the L train? where should citibike expand? where should schools expand?
CS Topics:
  • aquiring data: start with cleansed data in spread sheets, move to scraping websites (beautifulSoup, json), and finish with a crash course in SQL
  • analyzing data: when is an observed pattern statistically meaningful and when is it a coincidence?
  • visualizing data: graphing statistical data, geographic maps (linked hover maps with bokeh), abstracting to trees and graphs
  • side topics: tools to share, github, webpages, etc.
