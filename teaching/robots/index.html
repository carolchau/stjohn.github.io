Announcements:

  • The programming language for the course is Not Quite C (NQC), written by Dave Baum.
    Some useful NQC documents include:
    • NQC Programmer's Reference Sheet by Matthew Bates:
      2 pages with every NQC command and construct, very briefly described.
    • NQC Programmer's Guide by Dave Baum:
      62 page guide to programming in NQC.
    • NQC User Manual by Dave Baum:
      How to use the command line version of the NQC compiler.

Lab Information: 

Due to the small size of the computer labs:
  • Keep your robot turned off except when downloading a program. This is to conserve batteries and to keep others infra-red transmissions from corrupting programs on your robot.
  • Test programs in the hallway (or patio area for Cluster V).
  • Eating and drinking is fine in the hallway or patio, but not the lab itself.
  • For Cluster III: We share the storeroom in Kerr with the COSMOS staff. As a courtesy to them, all Lego pieces must be inside your blue box or plastic bin before putting them away in the storage area.
  • For Cluster V: do not bring boxes of Lego pieces into the computer lab. Use the hallway or patio for building or modifying your robot.  In addition to being our storeroom, Physics 112 is also an office for lecturers in the Physics department. As a courtesy to them, all robots and Lego pieces should be stored inside the plasic bins when in Physics 112.
