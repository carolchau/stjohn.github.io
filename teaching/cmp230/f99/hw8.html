Graded Problems

P16. Write a program that asks the user for information about their bank account, including their balance. The program will then ask for the current interest rate and print out how many years it takes for their account balance to double. The program should print out the balance every year (until their money doubles) and assumes that the interest is compounded yearly.

Here's a sample run:
Please enter your name: Bill Clinton
Please enter your address: 1600 Pennsylvania Ave.
Please enter your social security number: 123456789
Please enter your balance: 1000

What is the interest rate? 6

Your balance is at
Year 0: $1000
Year 1: $1060
Year 2: $1123.60
Year 3: $1191.016
Year 4: $1262.477
Year 5: $1338.2256
Year 6: $1418.5191
Year 7: $1503.6303
Year 8: $1593.8481
Year 9: $1689.479
Year 10: $1790.8477
Year 11: $1898.2986
Year 12: $2012.1965
In 12 years, your money has doubled.
(The words in bold were entered by the user.)

(Hint: Use a loop, and don't worry about formatting the salary (that is, how many places after the decimal are displayed) since we haven't talked about how to do that yet.)
