  • Catalog Description: CMP 230: Programming Methods I. 4 hours, 4 credits. (NOTE: For students who intend to major in computer science, mathematics or the sciences.) Formulation of algorithms suitable for computer implementation. Programming in a high-level language. Debugging techniques. PREREQ: Three years of Regents-level high school mathematics, or MAT 104.
  • Textbook: Java: An Introduction to Computer Science and Programming by Walter Savitch, Prentice Hall, 1999 (ISBN: 0-13-287426-1).
  • Programming Environment: JBuilder, available bundled with the book when purchased at the college bookstore, or from the department.
  • Grading: The grading for the course will be based on:
    • Programs: 20%
    • Midterm Exam 1: 25%
    • Midterm Exam 2: 25%
    • Final Exam: 30%
  • Exams: The exams will be
    • Midterm Exam 1: Wednesday, 29 September in G 227.
    • Midterm Exam 2: Wednesday, 3 November in G 227.
    Since the final is comprehensive, if you do better on it than on one of the midterm exams, the score on the final may replace one of the midterm scores. Note that the final will be long and difficult, so, it is an exceptionally bad idea to plan on taking advantage of this. There will be no makeup exams, and you must pass the final to pass the class.
  • Assignments: The programming assignments are available at the class website. You may turn in programs at any laboratory session, but no program will be accepted after the last laboratory session of the semester. If you wish to improve a grade on any program, you may resubmit it for grading.
    Full credit will only be given for those programs that run correctly and have good documentation and programming style.
  • Honor Code: You are encouraged to work together on the overall design of the programs and homework. However, for specific programs and homework assignments, all work must be your own.
  • Computer Accounts: The department does not provide computer accounts. You will need an account with e-mail access to turn in your homework. Accounts are available to all students at the Computer Center, or, if you already have another account, you may use that.
