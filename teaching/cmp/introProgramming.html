Introductory Programming

Computer Science addresses the fundamental issues of designing and implementing computer systems, of efficiently and correctly solving computational problems, of investigating new uses of computers, and of integrating information systems with organizations to improve their functioning. For all of these, programming is a fundamental skill. The computer science program offers several different entry points to programming:

  • CMP 230: A first programming course, taught in Python, and required for computer science, CGI, and mathematics major. The course focuses on programming basics, and also uses the Eclipse IDE in the latter half of the course.
    Strongly recommended for the quantitative biology minor and students in the sciences.
