Writing Up Answers

Each problem that you present on the chalkboard, you also need to write up and submit via the Dropbox on Blackboard. When you write up an answer, make sure
  • your name is on top of the first page, 
  • include the problem number (and page numbers, if relevant),
  • state the problem clearly written out (to make it easier to use for studying), and
  • use complete sentences.

Proofs

Like writing a paper, you need to present a convincing argument that is concise and easy to read.  The last part of Chapter 0 focuses on how to find proofs and how to write them down.  Some general rules to follow:
  • State what you're trying to prove, and if appropriate, the type of proof (i.e. proof by induction, proof by contradiction, etc.).  While neither of these is required, it makes it much easier for the reader to understand and follow what you're saying.
  • Use complete sentences in your proofs.  On the chalkboard, it's fine to just write down the main ideas, but when writing up problems formally, you must use proper English and full sentences.  Again, this is to make it easier to read and understand in the future.
  • Be neat.  Your proof needs to convince the reader that the mathematical statement in question is true.  Sloppiness makes it difficult to follow the details of the argument.
  • Be concise.  While this might seem contrary to the requirement to use complete sentences, it is possible to be brief and convey your ideas to the reader.  Include enough detail to get your ideas across, but try to avoid tangents and extra comments that might confuse the reader.
