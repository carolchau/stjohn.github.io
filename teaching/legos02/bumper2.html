Bumper Car 2

This is a simple vehicle with front and back bumpers. The drive train is fairly standard - tank treads driven by geared down (3:1) motors. The bumper design is interesting - the bumper pivots around an axle, and is kept in the extended position by use of a rubber band pulling down on the back side of the bumper. A small axle protrudes from the back of the bumper, and this presses against the touch sensor in the normal position. When an obstacle is hit, however, the bumper pivots and the small axle moves away from the touch sensor, thus breaking contact.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

NQC Source

/* bumper2.nqc
 * written by Dave Baum for NQC 2.0
 */
#define LEFT OUT_C
#define RIGHT OUT_A
#define FRONT SENSOR_1
#define BACK SENSOR_3

task main()
{
	SetSensor(FRONT, SENSOR_TOUCH);
	SetSensor(BACK, SENSOR_TOUCH);

	On(LEFT + RIGHT);

	while(true)
	{
		// wait until we hit something
		until(FRONT==0 || BACK==0);
		
		// reverse for a little
		Rev(LEFT+RIGHT);
		Wait(100);
		
		// pick new direction
		Fwd(LEFT);		
		Wait(Random(200));
		
		// resume straight
		Fwd(RIGHT);
	}
}
