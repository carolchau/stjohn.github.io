Playing Ball

Today's lab focues on several simple ways to play ball.  Tasks including "playing catch" and "making goals".  Decision, or if-statements, first used in the previous lab, will be the main programming construct used.

Design requirements: This lab assumes that the two motors are wired to output ports A and C. Further, each motor is attached to a means of locomotion (legs, treads, feet, etc.). The touch sensor is wired to sensor port 1 and is connected to a bumper.  You might want to invert the bumpers feelers to make it easier to corner or capture the ball.

Programs used: Today's lab uses the touch sensors and the bumpbot1.nqc from lecture.

Lab Set-up: This lab uses a half dozen small balls, and a small soccer goal.  Choose the ball that works best with your robot configuration.

Catching the Ball

The first task is to write a program that will a sound when a ball hits the bumper.  Depending on how your bumper is set up, choose the one that hits your bumper the best.  This will be a very short program with the main structure in the program is an if-statement.  See the NQC Reference Sheet for the different beeps and tones that the robots can make.

Now, take your program and have it repeatedly make a sound when a ball hits the bumper.  Look at how the bumpbot1.nqc repeats its task of backing up when it bumps something, and use that same idea for your program.

Throwing the Ball

The next task is to throw the ball back, after you catch it.  To throw it back, you should back up slightly (to get a running start), run forward the same amount, and stop abruptly. 

Playing Catch

Find a partner who has also completed the catching and throwing program, and set up your robots facing one
another.  Gently roll a ball to one of them.  The goal is to have them "play catch": throwing the ball and catching it at least 3 times.  You may need to adjust your programs to weaken or strengthen the robot's throws, so that your partner can catch your throws.

Making Goals

For the last program, your robot should stand in front of goal (on the black tape mark) and when a ball is passed to
your robot, it should push/kick the ball into the goal.  Your robot can come close, but should not enter the goal.
