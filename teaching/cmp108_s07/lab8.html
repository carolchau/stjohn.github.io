Animation Programming: Alice

Today's lab is the first on animation programming.  We will be using the Alice programming environment (http://www.alice.org).

Design requirements:  We will be using one of the built-in tutorials and the galleries (available on-line or loaded in from the CD).

Tutorial

Open the Alice animation system and choose "Open World" from the File Menu (this should automatically open when you open Alice).   Click on the "Tutorial" tab, and choose Tutorial 1.  Work through the entire tutorial.  Note more explicit directions on starting Alice can be found in Appendix A of the textbook.

Island Scene

Next, create an island scene, following the directions for Exercise 1 on p 16.   To start a new world, choose the "New World" option from the File Menu.  Save your world (we'll use it later in the course).

Winter Snowmen

In honor of all the snow we've had, we're now going to build a winter scene.  First, add two snowmen to a snowy scene (see Exercise 2, p 17 for details).  Save this, and then modify it to be a snowpeople pile (see Exercise 3, p 17 for details).

Finishing up for the Day

To get credit for this laboratory, you need to show and explain your programs to Prof. St. John, as well as demonstrate that they run using your robot.

We must be out of the computer laboratory by 12:30pm.  As such, if you are not ready to demonstrate your work by 12:15pm, you can do so in the next laboratory.  Before leaving the room, make sure that all computers and monitors are turned off, that any paper and trash have been removed, and that all chairs are returned to their proper places.  Also, make sure you pack up your IR tower in your shoe box, since they're difficult to replace.
