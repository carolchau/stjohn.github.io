More on Loops

Today's lab focuses on definite and indefinite loops.  We will be using the Alice programming environment (http://www.alice.org).

Design requirements:  We will be using one of the built-in tutorials and the galleries (available on-line or loaded in from the CD).

Animations

Loops are a standard programming construct and work similarly in Alice as they did with the robots.  Today's programs demonstrate the Loop construct (much like a for loop in NQC) and the While
construct (almost identical to the while loop in NQC).

For today's lab, do the following animations:
  • Square bunny hop (Chapter 7, Exercise 2):  The main point of this exercise is to have a creature (bunny or one of your choice), trace out a square.  If you choose the bunny, you can use the method described in Section 7-1 to make the bunny perform one hop.  Your creature needs to trace out (by hopping, walking, driving forward, etc) a side of the square, then make a quarter turn revolution.  As with the robots, this is then put in a loop.
  • Frog and Ladybug (Chapter 7, Exercise 5):  For this exercise, you need two creatures, one that is dragged around by the mouse, the other that tries to follow.  You can use the suggested ladybug and frog, or creatures or your choice. 
  • Wind-up Penguin (Chapter 7, Exercise 10):  For this animation, a creature (the penguin or one of your choice) waddles around the world while the wind-up key turns.  Remember that you can set the key's vehicle property to your creature, so that the key stays with the creature as it moves.
