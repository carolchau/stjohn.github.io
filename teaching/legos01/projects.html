Project Ideas

Cluster V: Computers and Robots
COSMOS Program, UC Davis
13 July 2001

Each cluster has a final project that will be part of the closing event on Saturday, 4 August. For our cluster, you have the choice of doing a computational physics project or building a robot and accompanying programs.

If you choose to build a robot:

  • Your project can have up to 5 programs (the total size of all loaded programs cannot exceed 6K).
  • Different lego configurations for different programs are possible, but you must be able to convert from one to another quickly.
  • Designs need to be submitted during the second week, so, we can arrange for special parts (if needed).
Here's some possible ideas:
  • a robot with an arm (see Chapter 5 of the Unofficial Guide)
  • a brick sorter
  • a jukebox that plays songs on request
  • a robot that navigates a maze using the light sensor and bumpers
  • a garbage truck
  • an aerial tram
  • a robot that plays tic-tac-toe
  • a team of robots that cooperates in completing a task (like playing tag, relay race, moving an object, etc.)
  • a clock
  • a plotter
  • a scanner
