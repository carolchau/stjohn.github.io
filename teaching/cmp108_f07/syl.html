  • Description:  Programming for non-Computer Science majors. 4 hours, (2 lecture, 2 lab) 3 credits. Implementation of basic programming constructs using robots, designing of simple video games and creation of elementary web pages.
  • Textbook: There is one required textbook: 
    • Learning to Program with Alice by Dann, Cooper, &  Pausch
    and one recommended textbook:
    • Dave Baum's Definitive Guide to Lego Mindstorm Robots
  • Grading: The grading for the course will be based on:
    • Computer Laboratory Exercises: 35%
    • Written Work and Quizzes: 30%
    • Final Project: 25%
    • Class Participation: 10% 
  • Computer Laboratory Exercises:  Laboratory exercises are posted on the class website, usually the week before class.  They reinforce concepts covered in lecture and should be completed within 3 weeks of the original laboratory.  Note that as the semester progresses, the exercises will take design and programming outside of class to complete.  To receive credit for an laboratory exercise, you must demonstrate the working robot or Alice world, the associated programs, and explain how they work.
  • Written Work & Quizzes: There will be written homework and quizzes based on the readings and lectures for class.  We will use the on-line Blackboard system for both written exercises and quizzes.  You can resubmit written homework and quizzes for regrading within 3 weeks of the original due date.  See the course webpage for more details about homework and quizzes.    
  • Final Project:  This course will have a significant project of your own design or a 15-20 page term paper.  Your project should include a robot designed to solve a given problem, associated programs, and a webpage documenting how to use and maintain your robot.  More information about the project will be available on the course website.
  • Class Participation:  Participation in classroom discussion and computer laboratories are an integral part of the course. 
  • Honor Code: You are encouraged to work together on the overall design of the programs and homework. However, for specific programs and homework assignments, all work must be your own.  You are responsible for knowing and following Lehman's academic integrity code (available from the Undergraduate Bulletin, Graduate Bulletin, or the Office of Academic Standards and Evaluations).
    All incidents of cheating will be reported to the Vice President of Student Affairs.
  • Computer Access: Part of this course will use university computer laboratories.  These machines are for work related to this course only and a code of conduct applies to computer use in the department and on-campus.  Misusing university computers could result in losing your computer access for the rest of the term, making it exceedingly difficult to complete this course.
