Format

Quiz 2 consists of two parts:
  1. A multiple choice quiz on the reading, and
  2. A short essay based on the reading.
For Part 1, the Blackboard system randomly chooses from a pool of questions to make the quiz.  If you are not happy with your score, you can take the quiz over, and a new set of random questions will be generated.

For Part 2, you need to enter your essay answer to the chosen question.  Since there's several to choose from, make sure to clearly mark at the beginning which question you have chosen to answer.  Spelling and grammar do count, so, please use a spell-checker before entering your essay.  Since Blackboard will only accept plain text, it is probably easiest to write your essay in a word processor and then cut-and-paste it into the quiz window.

Topics

The questions in Part 1 are straightforward and based on the reading.  The best way to study for this is to do the reading.

For Part 2, choose one of the following questions and write a short essay.  Your essay should be about a page long and clearly address the question.  The questions below are from p 132 of the text:

1. The overview of this unit mentions Michael Crichton's latest novel, Prey.  The physicist Freeman Dyson reviews this novel in the February 13, 2002 issue of The New York Review of Books (http://www.nybooks.com/articles/16053).  Do you agree with what he has to say about the threats that technology hold for us?

2. Kevin Mitnick, author of "Are You the Weak Link?" and a convicted computer criminal, is now a computer security consultant.  Can you imagine a bank robber going to work for the banking industry?  How about a car thief consulting for the police department?  Do these examples say anything about the way we view white-collar crime in general and computer crime in particular?  Explain.

3. Do you feel safe giving your credit card number to merchants over the Web?  Find out how (or if) your number is protected from criminals who might traffic between you and the merchant?
