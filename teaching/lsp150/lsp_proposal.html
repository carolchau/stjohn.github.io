For Lehman Scholars students, "seminar" means "a place where seeds
are planted." Seminars are expected to be discussion sessions;
they are not intended to be lecture performances.

 
Title of seminar:                 Computers and Society

Day and Time Requested:     A 3-hour block of time on Tuesday, Wednesday, or Friday
                                           or Thursday mornings

Description of seminar:    

 
Computers have become an integral part of our daily lives.  In this
seminar, we propose to look at the effects of computers on society,
in particular the incorporation and long-reaching consequences of
robotics on how we interact with the world.  To give depth and
context to this discussion, the course will include a hands-on
component of designing and programming Legos Mindstorm Robots.
The basics of locomotion, design, as well as introductory programming
(in a variant of the popular C-programming language, customized for
the robots) will be taught.  As part of the seminar, students will
design and implement a robotics project of their choosing.  Students
in the course will write traditional essays, as well as a technical guides
to using their robotics projects.
 
Your reasons for offering your seminar:

I would like to encourage more of the strong students at Lehman,
especially women, to consider majoring or minoring in computer
science.  Despite the overwhelming number of women enrolled at
Lehman, few choose to major in computer science.  I hope by presenting
an interesting and fun introduction to the major and its place in society,
students who might otherwise be put off will consider taking future
courses in computer science and other technology related fields.

 
How will students benefit from your seminar?:

The proposed course is aimed at bright students from all fields.
Those who would benefit most would be lower division students who
haven't had a chance to take a technology-related course.  Since
the emphasis of the course is on topics not covered at Lehman in
the math or computer science curriculum, lower-division students
from technology related fields would also enjoy the course.
