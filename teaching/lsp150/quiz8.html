Format

Quiz 2 consists of two parts:
  1. A multiple choice quiz on the reading, and
  2. A short essay based on the reading.
For Part 1, the Blackboard system randomly chooses from a pool of questions to make the quiz.  If you are not happy with your score, you can take the quiz over, and a new set of random questions will be generated.

For Part 2, you need to enter your essay answer to the chosen question.  Since there's several to choose from, make sure to clearly mark at the beginning which question you have chosen to answer.  Spelling and grammar do count, so, please use a spell-checker before entering your essay.  Since Blackboard will only accept plain text, it is probably easiest to write your essay in a word processor and then cut-and-paste it into the quiz window.

Topics

The questions in Part 1 are straightforward and based on the reading.  The best way to study for this is to do the reading.

For Part 2, choose one of the following questions and write a short essay.  Your essay should be about a page long and clearly address the question.  The questions below are from p 174 of the text:

1. The article "Immigration and the Global IT Work Force" talks about a shortage of technical workers.  The government statistics cited come from 1998.  Has the situation changed since then?  What is the current occupational outlook for tech workers?

2. Use the Internet to examine the arguments for H1-B visas to the United States.  What is your position on granting temporary visas to technical workers?

3. The writers of the article "Wiring the Wilderness in Alaska and the Yukon," say "so far it is not bringing wholesale prosperity to any village we could find."  Is this surprising to you?  Why or why not?

4.  It is often argued that information technologies have the potential to democratize polotically oppressive regimes.  Do you agree?  Use the information in "The Quiet Revolution" and "Dot Com for Dictators" in formulating your answer.
