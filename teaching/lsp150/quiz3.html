Format

Quiz 2 consists of two parts:
  1. A multiple choice quiz on the reading, and
  2. A short essay based on the reading.
For Part 1, the Blackboard system randomly chooses from a pool of questions to make the quiz.  If you are not happy with your score, you can take the quiz over, and a new set of random questions will be generated.

For Part 2, you need to enter your essay answer to the chosen question.  Since there's several to choose from, make sure to clearly mark at the beginning which question you have chosen to answer.  Spelling and grammar do count, so, please use a spell-checker before entering your essay.  Since Blackboard will only accept plain text, it is probably easiest to write your essay in a word processor and then cut-and-paste it into the quiz window.

Topics

The questions in Part 1 are straightforward and based on the reading.  The best way to study for this is to do the reading.

For Part 2, choose one of the following questions and write a short essay.  Your essay should be about a page long and clearly address the question.  The questions below are modified from p 16 of the text:


1. The overview to this units says, "you may be surprised to learn... that some economists doubt the relationship between increseard output per worker, that is, productivity, and information technology." Are you surprised? Things change quickly. What do economists say now?

2. Does the assertion that up to 40 percent of all e-mail traffic is now spam seem too large? Use the internet to find the source of this figure. Do you beleive the estimates for the future growth in spam? Why or why not?

3. Should spam and all unsolicited email be outlawed? Should all unsolicited US mail be outlawed? Discuss the similarities and difference between your answers to these questions.
