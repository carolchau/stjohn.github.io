    Problem 10: Given the relation schema R(A,B,C,D) with the functional dependencies: AB->C, BC->D, CD->A, and AD->B.

    a) Indicate all the BCNF violations:

    First, find the closures of each subset of attributes. Note that the closure of the singletons are just the singletons:
    A+ = A, B+ = B, C+ = C, and D+ = D.
    For the subsets of size two, break into two groups: those who are the left hand side of a functional dependency (those turn out to be keys) and the rest whose closure are themselves:
    AB+ = BC+ = CD+ = AD+ = all attributes
    AC+ = AC, BD+ = BD.
    The subsets of size three are all extensions of the keys of size two (ABC, ABD, ACD, and BCD) are superkeys.

    So, for each of the functional dependencies above, the left hand side is a key, so they are all in BCNF.

    The question also says to consider the derived dependencies, that is all functional dependencies that follow from the ones given. Note that any derived functional dependency must start with a left hand side of those given. Since the left hand sides are all keys, any derived key must also be in BCNF.

    b) Decompose the relations, as necessary, into a collection of relations that are in BCNF.

    R is in BCNF, so, no decomposition is needed.
