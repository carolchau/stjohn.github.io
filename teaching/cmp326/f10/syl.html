CMP 326: Programming Methods II
Lehman College, City University of New York

Instructor: Dr. Katherine St. John

Course Times: Tuesdays, Thursdays 9-10:40pm
  • Description: Continuation of parameter passing with a focus on devising function definitions and tracing recursive calls. Sorting and searching algorithms and a comparison of their performance. GUI programming. Threads, Exceptions and Exception Handling. Object Oriented Programming techniques.
  • Prerequisite: A grade of a B- or better in CMP 230 or departmental permission.
  • Textbook: The required textbook is:
    • Java: An Introduction to Problem Solving and Programming by Savitch and Carrano, Fifth Edition, Pearson, 2009, ISBN 0-13-607225-9.
      • It is available for sale from the campus bookstore or from on-line booksellers such as Amazon or Powells. You can also rent the book for the semester from Chegg.com.
  • Grading: The grading for the course will be based on:
    • Problem Sets: 15%
    • Written Work & Quizzes: 10%
    • Programming Assessment 1: 10%
    • Programming Assessment 2: 10%
    • Final Programming Assessment: 15%
    • Midterm Exam 1: 10%
    • Midterm Exam 2: 10%
    • Final Exam: 20%
  • Computer Laboratory Exercises: Laboratory exercises are posted on the class website, usually the week before class. They reinforce concepts covered in lecture and are usually due within 3 weeks of the original laboratory. Note that as the semester progresses, the exercises will take design and programming outside of class to complete. To receive credit for an laboratory exercise, you must demonstrate the associated programs, and explain how they work.
  • Written Work & Quizzes: There will be written homework and quizzes based on the readings and lectures for class. We will use the on-line Blackboard system for both written exercises and quizzes. You can resubmit written homework and quizzes for regrading within 3 weeks of the original due date. See the course webpage for more details about homework and quizzes.
  • Programming Assessments: Since a major component of this course is learning to program, there will be three programming assessments to test programming. These will take place on:
    • Tuesday, 7 September
    • Thursday, 28 October
    • Thursday, 10 December
    Since the final assessment is comprehensive, if you do better on it than on the earlier assessments the score may replace the earlier scores. There will be no makeup assessments, and you must pass the final assessment to pass the class.
  • Exams: There will be two midterm exams and a final exam for this course. The exams are:
    • Thursday, 7 October
    • Thursday, 11 November
    • Tuesday, 16 December
    Since the final is comprehensive, if you do better on it than on one of the midterm exams, the score on the final may replace one of the midterm scores. Note that the final will be long and difficult, so, it is an exceptionally bad idea to plan on taking advantage of this. There will be no makeup exams, and you must pass the final to pass the class.
  • Honor Code: You are encouraged to work together on the overall design of the programs and homework. However, for specific programs and homework assignments, all work must be your own. You are responsible for knowing and following Lehman's academic integrity code (available from the Undergraduate Bulletin, Graduate Bulletin, or the Office of Academic Standards and Evaluations).
    All incidents of cheating will be reported to the Vice President of Student Affairs.
  • Computer Access: Part of this course will use university computer laboratories. These machines are for work related to this course only and a code of conduct applies to computer use in the department and on-campus.  Misusing university computers could result in losing your computer access for the rest of the term, making it exceedingly difficult to complete this course.
